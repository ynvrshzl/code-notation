### Definition
"d" is the symbolic equivalent of **description.**

### Reasoning
Classically speaking, the descript factor of any set transformation, is that which explains it's function, at the cognitive human level of reasoning. For both the authoring of systems, and interpretation via analysis. 

### Significance
This is a much a computationally observable part of systems, as much as it is a human level of reasoning.

In essence, this symbolic unit captures the idea _"the system/code should explain itself."_ That is both technically and lexcially incomplete and wrong. The "system" can't prove itself [^1], it requires external operation
