### Definition
"d" is the symbolic equivalent of **description.**

### Reasoning
Classically speaking, the descript factor of any set transformation, is that which explains it's function, at the cognitive human level of reasoning. For both the authoring of systems, and interpretation via analysis. 

### Significance
In essence, this symbolic unit captures the idea "the system/code should explain itself." That is both technically and lexcially incomplete and wrong. The "system" can't prove itself [^1], it requires external operation 

### Formulaic/Algebraic equation

$$
\sum (o = x(i)) = d
$$

To reason about the description/purpose of a transformation model, we translate the sum of the transformation, as a general note of function. This actively compresses a transformational step, as a historical/generational process.

> e.g., instead of _"const x = 32;"_ we compute _"the sum of this operation"_... which becomes the pragmatic operation: _"storage of number **'32'** as a variable **'x'**."_ Effectively increasing surface area, but at a cognitive process.
